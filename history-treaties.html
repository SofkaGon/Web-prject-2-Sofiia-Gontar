TREATY

Treaties are legal agreements between groups of people, such as countries, nations or smaller groups that share common goal. Trieties are important part of Canadian history. There are many important trieties helping us to live in peace with eachother, many of which were set by the Crown with Indigenous people. Trieties differentiate from other agreements by being "binding" were both parties should do they're part of the promise. That's why is important to remember set treaties and fullfill them to the best of our ability.

TREATY ONE

First numbered treaty, Treaty 1 was nagotiated in 1871, by Crown and the Anishinabek and Swampy Cree nations of southern Manitoba. Treaty was signed at "Stone Fort" or as known now Fort Garry. As announced by Queen Victoria, the treaty were to be fair for both native people and Crown. In the treaty promise is written "Each band was to receive a reserve large enough to provide 160 acres for each family of five (or in like proportion for smaller or larger families). Each man, woman, and child was to be given a gratuity — or one-time payment — of three dollars, and a yearly annuity totalling $15 per family of five. The government also agreed to maintain a school on each reserve and to prohibit the introduction or sale of liquor on reserves."

SHARING THE LAND

Huge focus of the treaty was on land division, use and ownership. Land should have been shared accordingly and Indigenous people should have the ownership of the land and it's recources. They were promised that "Your Great Mother, therefore, will lay aside for you ‘lots’ of land to be used by you and your children forever. She will not allow the white men to intrude upon these lots. She will make rules to keep them for you, so that as long as the sun shall shine, there shall be no Indian who has not a place that he can call his home […]". But as time passed many Indigenous people were resetled to worse and worse reserves, were it was harder to hunt and farm. It also took them from their native own land.

HELPING EACHOTHER

In this treaty Crown offered additional help to Indigenous people “the Queen was willing to help the Indians in every way, and that besides giving them land and annuities, she would give them a school and a schoolmaster for each reserve, and for those who desired to cultivate the soil ploughs and harrows would be provided on the reserves.” As greatly as it sounded, 'help' given was not as ideal. Indigenous people saw worth in education and therefore agreed to schooling to learn new perspectives. That mentionet education quickly became part of residential schools, forced assimilation, mistreatment, and saperation of families.

TRUTH AND RECONCILIATION

In recent years, after many years of hardships that Indigenous people faced and Canadian government hid, truth and reconciliation commision, day and acts were created. The day of September the 30th or orange shirt day, is a reminder of hardships, cruelty and unfairness many indigenous people faced. It's also a day to strive for friendship, reconciliation and remember the truth. Truth and Reconciliation is one of recent ways to remind about the past, advocate for change and recompensation, and to make fair treaties and fair treatment.
